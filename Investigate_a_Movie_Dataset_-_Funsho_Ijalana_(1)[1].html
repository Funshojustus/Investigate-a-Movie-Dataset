Introduction¶

Dataset Description¶

This data set contains information about 10,000 movies collected from The Movie Database (TMDb), including user ratings and revenue.

The dataset contains different variables like id, imdb_id, popularity, budget, revenue, original_title, cast, homepage, director, tagline, keywords, overview, runtime, genres, production_companies, release_date, vote_count, vote_average, release_year, budget_adj (adjusted budget) and revenue_adj (adjusted revenue).

The final two columns ending with _adj show the budget and revenue of the associated movie in terms of 2010 dollars, accounting for inflation over time.

There are 10,866 rows and 21 columns in the dataset.

Question(s) for Analysis¶

  1. Which genres are most popular from year to year?

  2. What kinds of properties (genre) are associated with movies that have high revenues?

  3. Has the number of movies released increased overtime since 1960?

  4. Is there a correlation between Movies runtime and revenue?

Conclusions¶

Action, Adventure, Science Fiction, Drama and Thriller are the genres that are most popular from year to year.

Action, Adventure, Science Fiction, Thriller, Fantasy and Drama are the genres that have high revenues.

The number of movies released increased overtime since 1960 till date

There is no significant correlation between the Movie runtime and Revenue as some high revenue movies had lower runtime, and vice versa

A limitation of my exploration is the absence of actual number of viewers or individuals that rated the movies to accurately predict the correlation.
